Equality

Equality refers to the right of different groups of people to have a similar social position and receive the same treatment.

In the 21st century, numerous forms of equality have emerged, including gender equality and advocacy for women, as well as racial equality. As we strive toward these goals, we believe in the power of theater to bridge gaps and foster understanding to create a community with improved awareness and increased acceptance between individuals.

Equity

Equity refers to the principle of being fair and just, adjusting for the imbalance.

From an education level, means ensuring that all students have access to the resources and opportunities they need to succeed, regardless of their backgrounds or circumstances. We can recognize and address systemic barriers that may affect marginalized groups and increase awareness to create a learning environment where every student has the chance to thrive, fostering not only academic success but also social and emotional growth.

AD Appreciation Program

During the year, our S5 students have participated in AD lessons to learn skills regarding voice acting, drama directing and producing, sound effects and design, makeup, and scene drawing. To put learnt knowledge into use, students are required to create a radio drama, and a variety of activities will be organized to appreciate the effort and dedication students have put in. These will include online voting for their radio drama, a special Drama Night event, and opportunities for exchanges with students from other secondary schools.
